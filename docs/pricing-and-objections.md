That’s your “buffer” already baked into the bundle — not a random discount.

### Comparison anchors (always)

| Alternative | Rough cost | Gap |
|-------------|------------|-----|
| Part-time receptionist | ~$1,500–$1,800/mo (20–25 hrs) | Only hours, no web, no reviews |

**Break-even story (use in every audit):**  
One recovered $500–$1,000 job ≈ almost a full month of Reviews, or a chunk of Voice/Bundle.  
Conservative: **2–3 booked jobs/month** from previously missed calls pays for Voice; **1 solid job** often covers Reviews.

## 2. Setup fees — what you charge (and what you don’t)

### Current rule (locked)

| Item | Policy |
|------|--------|
| **Setup / onboarding fee** | **$0 listed** — setup is **included** in first paid month |
| **First month** | **Paid in full, in advance** (unlocks setup) |
| **Net 30 / 90** | **No** |
| **Hardware / software install fee** | **None** (you configure; they forward phone / give access) |
| **Custom proposal / RFP** | **No** — productized SKUs only |

**What “setup” means (say this):**  
> “No separate setup fee. First month paid starts a short intake + configuration. Live in days, not months. One 20–30 minute call.”

**Your real cost of setup (internal — don’t itemize for client):**  
Time for script, hours, calendar, Twilio/Vapi wiring, testing. That’s why **pay-first** exists: no free custom projects.

### Optional setup fee later (only if you need a buffer)

Use **only** if: tire-kickers, heavy hand-holding, or you raise list prices.

| Option | Amount | When to use |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **A — Keep $0 setup** (default) | $0 | First 5–10 clients; triangle GTM |
| **B — Soft setup** | **$250–$500** one-time | “Rush” or multi-location later |
| **C — Setup credit** | $500 setup **waived** if they pay Bundle annual? | Avoid for now (you’re MTM) |

**Recommendation now:** stay **$0 setup**, protect margin with **no free pilots**, **pay first**, and **bundle as default**.

## 3. Buffer, discounts, and “savings” you can offer

### Built-in buffer (preferred — don’t discount list)

1. **List vs floor** (never go below floor without a written reason)

| SKU | List (quote) | Floor (hard no below) | Notes |
|-----|--------------|----------------------|--------|
| Reviews | $400 | **$300** | Site low end |
| Receptionist | $650 | **$500** | Site low end |
| Voice | $1,100 | **$800** | Site low end |
| Bundle | $1,500 | **$1,500** | **Do not discount bundle** — walk to single SKU |

2. **Time buffer** — first 30 days = “prove the leak,” not free months.  
3. **Scope buffer** — only paid SKU; extras = next month add-on.

### Savings you *can* offer (without looking cheap)

| Lever | Script | Cost to you |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| **Start smaller** | “Start Reviews at $400; add Voice in 30–60 days.” | Lower revenue now, higher close |
| **Bundle vs separate** | “All three is $1,500 vs ~$2,150 separate — save ~$650/mo.” | Already in price card |
| **Waive rush** | “Normal setup in X days; no rush fee.” | $0 |
| **Month-to-month** | “No annual lock-in — that’s the savings vs enterprise.” | $0 |
| **Free 15-min audit** | Not free software — free diagnosis | Time only |
| **First month only list** | Never “2 months free” | Avoid |

### Discounts to refuse

- Ongoing % off Bundle  
- Free month to “try”  
- Net 30  
- Custom price “because competitor quoted lower” without a **smaller SKU**  
- Free website / free ads / free unlimited rewrites

## 4. Am I too expensive?

### Short answer

**Not for the right ICP** (5–15 person HVAC/plumbing/electrical, phone-driven, 209 triangle).  
**Yes for** tire-kickers, solo flippers with no call volume, or buyers who want a $49/mo chatbot.

### How to know in the room

| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| They can name missed calls / tickets | Price is about **ROI**, not expensive |
| “How much for a chatbot?” | Wrong frame — reframe to **front desk / recovered jobs** |
| Compares to free Google Business tools | Education needed; may not be a fit |
| Compares to $15/hr hire | Use part-time hire math |
| Ghost after price | Often **process** (pay-first) not price — follow once, then park |

### One-liner when “too expensive” hits

> “Fair. Compared to a hire, it’s usually less. Compared to doing nothing, one recovered job a month often covers it. If budget’s tight we start at Reviews for $400 — not a free version of the whole front desk.”

| Needs enterprise SLA / multi-state | “Not the fit today — productized local crews.” |

## 6. Objection → response bank (drill these)

### “Too expensive.”

> “Compared to what — a hire or a missed job? Bundle is $1,500; a part-time person is often that before taxes and still can’t cover nights. If $1,500 is a stretch, we start Reviews at $400 and add Voice when you see the leak stop.”

### “I need to think about it.”

> “Totally. The audit numbers are yours either way. What would make this a clear yes or no by Friday — price, trust, or timing?”

*(Park if vague. One follow-up max.)*

### “My cousin’s kid can set up a chatbot.”

> “Chatbots answer FAQs. This is a **front desk**: qualify, book, notify you, review ask after the job. Different job than a widget.”

### “We already have an answering service.”

> “They take messages. We book and text you with the job details — and we grow Google reviews. A lot of owners keep overflow on answering service and put **after-hours** on Voice first.”

### “What if the AI messes up?”

> “It’s trained on your services; edge cases go to a human follow-up path. You get recordings/transcripts. Month-to-month — you’re not stuck.”

### “Can you do it cheaper if I pay cash / annual?”

> “Price is the package, not the payment method. Cash/Zelle is fine for convenience; it doesn’t change the SKU.”

### “Setup seems like a lot of work for me.”

> “You do one short call and the setup form. We configure. No code, no hardware.”

### “I’ll get back to you after busy season.”

> “Busy season is when missed calls hurt most. Even Reviews alone compounds while you’re slammed. Want a small start now?”

### “Smith.ai / [big brand] is cheaper / bigger.”

> “They serve a wide market. I productize for **Central Valley trades** — local, month-to-month, all three systems in one stack. If you want a national platform, go national. If you want a local front desk that shows up, that’s Vox.”

### Silent rejection / ghost

1. One value follow-up (audit summary + one CTA).  
2. Then quarterly check-in or stop.  
Don’t chase.

## 9. Where the money goes (you vs tools) — no client “split”

### What the client pays (no hidden charges)

| Line | Policy |
|------|--------|
| Monthly SKU | One number (e.g. Bundle **$1,500/mo**) |
| Setup fee | **$0 listed** — included in first paid month |
| Usage overages | **None on your price card** (you absorb tool usage inside the flat fee) |
| “Platform fee” / “API surcharge” | **Do not add** — looks like bait-and-switch |
| Partner revenue share | **None** — Vox is your company; tools are your vendors |

**What you tell clients:**  
> “One monthly price. Setup is included. No per-minute invoice from me, no surprise software fees. Month-to-month.”

**What is true internally:**  
You pay Grok, Twilio, Vapi, Vercel, etc. out of **your** pocket. That is **COGS**, not a line item you pass through (unless you later productize “overage” — not recommended for early GTM).

There is **no split** with Smith, Twilio, or xAI. Vendors bill **you**. Client bills **you** only.

```
Client pays:     $1,500  (example Bundle)
                 ──────
Tools (COGS):    ~$30–$250  (typical small crew; see below)
Your time:       setup hours once + light support
Stripe/Zelle fee:~3% if card
                 ──────
Your gross:      most of the $1,500  (often ~85–98% after tools at normal volume)
```

### Rough monthly tool costs (order of magnitude — update from real bills)

Assumptions: one contractor client, moderate use. Prices move — check vendor dashboards.

| Tool | What for | Rough cost |
|------|----------|------------|
| **xAI Grok API** | Receptionist chat + Voice brain | Often **a few $ to low tens $/mo** per client at chat volumes; more if heavy Voice minutes on a pricey model. Prefer **Grok Fast / cheaper tier** for production volume. |
| **Twilio SMS** | Review texts, owner alerts, booking SMS | ~**$0.008/segment** US → e.g. 200 texts ≈ **$1.60**; 1,000 texts ≈ **$8** + ~**$1–2/mo** per number |
| **Twilio / Telnyx voice** | Minutes if not only Vapi | ~**$0.008–0.015/min** inbound US (ballpark) |
| **Vapi** (when Voice live) | Agent runtime | Platform often ~**$0.05/min** + model/STT/TTS/telephony on top → real all-in often ~**$0.15–0.35/min** |
| **Vercel / Formspree / Google** | Hosting, forms, sheet | **$0–low** at your scale |
| **Your labor** | Setup 2–6 hrs once; support | Not a vendor bill — opportunity cost |

**Voice example (illustrative):**  
100 min of answered calls/mo × ~$0.25 all-in ≈ **$25** tools.  
500 min × $0.25 ≈ **$125**.  
Still small vs **$1,100 Voice** or **$1,500 Bundle**.

**Receptionist-only example:**  
Hundreds of chat turns/mo on a cheap Grok tier often stays **under ~$10–40** unless volume explodes.

**Reviews-only example:**  
SMS for a crew doing 40–80 jobs/mo is often **under ~$5–15** in Twilio.

→ **Most of the price is yours** (labor, risk, local service, productization). Tools are usually a **small %** at early client volumes.

### Guardrails

- Floor prices protect against unprofitable “wins.”  
- Bundle at $1,500 stays healthy if you don’t over-customize.  
- If COGS rise, **raise list** — don’t add surprise surcharges mid-contract.  
- Track real bills monthly: xAI + Twilio + Vapi per client.  
- Cap abuse (rate limits, fair-use: e.g. “normal contractor volume; extreme call centers quoted custom”).
