## Am I too expensive?

**Not for the right buyer**  
5–15 person HVAC / plumbing / electrical · phone-driven · Turlock–Modesto–Manteca triangle.

**Yes for**  
Tire-kickers · no real call volume · “I want a $49 chatbot.”

| Signal in the room | Meaning |
|--------------------|---------|
| Can name missed calls / ticket size | Talk **ROI**, not apologize |
| “How much for a chatbot?” | Wrong frame → **front desk / recovered jobs** |
| Compares to free Google tools | Educate or not a fit |
| Compares to $15/hr hire | Part-time hire math |
| Ghost after price | Often pay-first friction · follow once, then park |

**One-liner:**  
> “Fair. Compared to a hire, it’s usually less. Compared to doing nothing, one recovered job a month often covers it. If budget’s tight we start Reviews at $400 — not a free version of the whole front desk.”

## Objection scripts (drill these)

### “Too expensive.”

> “Compared to what — a hire or a missed job? Bundle is $1,500; a part-time person is often that before taxes and still can’t cover nights. If $1,500 is a stretch, we start Reviews at $400 and add Voice when you see the leak stop.”

### “I need to think about it.”

> “Totally. The audit numbers are yours either way. What would make this a clear yes or no by Friday — price, trust, or timing?”

### “My cousin’s kid can set up a chatbot.”

> “Chatbots answer FAQs. This is a **front desk**: qualify, book, notify you, review ask after the job. Different job than a widget.”

### “We already have an answering service.”

> “They take messages. We book and text you with the job details — and we grow Google reviews. A lot of owners keep overflow on answering service and put **after-hours** on Voice first.”

### “What if the AI messes up?”

> “It’s trained on your services; edge cases go to a human follow-up path. You get recordings or transcripts. Month-to-month — you’re not stuck.”

### “Can you do it cheaper if I pay cash / annual?”

> “Price is the package, not the payment method. Cash or Zelle is fine for convenience; it doesn’t change the package.”

### “Setup seems like a lot of work for me.”

> “You do one short call and the setup form. We configure. No code, no hardware.”

### “I’ll get back to you after busy season.”

> “Busy season is when missed calls hurt most. Even Reviews alone compounds while you’re slammed. Want a small start now?”

### “Smith.ai / [big brand] is cheaper / bigger.”

> “They serve a wide market. I productize for **Central Valley trades** — local, month-to-month, all three systems in one stack. If you want a national platform, go national. If you want a local front desk that shows up, that’s Vox.”

### Ghost / silent no

1. One value follow-up (audit summary + one CTA).  
2. Then quarterly check-in or stop.  
Don’t chase.

## Legit “savings” framing (not discounts)

| Frame | Script angle |
|-------|----------------|
| Bundle vs separate | “$1,500 vs about $2,150 if sold alone.” |
| $0 setup | “Included in first month.” |
| Month-to-month | “No annual trap.” |
| Start small | “Reviews at $400, add Voice in 30–60 days.” |
| Free audit | Diagnosis only — not free software |

**Never give away:** free product months · free custom code · free websites.
